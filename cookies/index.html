Cookie Policy

This Cookie Policy explains how cookie-like technologies, first-party consent storage, analytics, and browser monitoring work on alex-tsi.dev.

Last updated: April 11, 2026

1. What this policy covers

This policy applies to cookies, pixels, local storage entries, browser identifiers, and similar technologies used in connection with this website, including the first-party consent mechanism, analytics, and performance monitoring tools.

2. What cookies and similar technologies are

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. Similar technologies may also store or read information from your browser or device, or may rely on unique identifiers generated through browser interactions.

3. Categories of technologies used on this website

The website uses the following categories of technologies:

  • Strictly necessary technologies: used for core website operation, security, traffic management, and storage of your consent preferences.
  • Analytics technologies: used to understand how visitors use the website, which pages are viewed, how users navigate, and how content performs. For this website, that category includes Google Analytics 4 after consent.
  • Performance and diagnostics technologies: used to measure availability, investigate technical errors, analyze browser-side performance, and maintain service reliability. For this website, that category includes New Relic browser monitoring after consent.

4. Consent model

This website uses a consent-first model for non-essential technologies. Analytics and browser monitoring technologies should not be activated until the relevant consent choice has been made through the website's consent mechanism.

Strictly necessary technologies may still be used without prior consent where permitted by applicable law because they support core website operation, security, or storage of your consent choice.

On first load, the website presents a short consent message with an Accept button. Selecting Accept records consent for non-essential cookies, analytics, and browser monitoring. After the initial choice, the Cookie Settings control in the footer lets you reopen the preferences dialog and update the saved categories.

5. How these technologies may be used

The website uses cookies and similar technologies to:

  • remember your consent choices;
  • maintain secure and stable website operation;
  • measure website traffic and engagement trends after consent;
  • identify broken pages, performance issues, and browser errors after consent;
  • improve content, navigation, and technical reliability.

6. Managing your preferences

The website's first-load consent message records your initial choice through the Accept button. After that, you can manage your saved categories through the Cookie Settings control shown in the footer. You may also restrict or delete cookies through your browser settings, although doing so may affect certain website functions.

If you withdraw consent, non-essential analytics and browser monitoring should stop loading for future interactions once your updated choice has been applied. If a previously loaded browser monitoring script needs to be stopped, the change may be applied on page reload.

7. Third-party processing and international access

Google Analytics 4 and New Relic browser monitoring involve third-party service providers that process device, usage, diagnostics, or performance information after consent. Some of these providers or their sub-processors may operate outside the European Economic Area.

The website's remote front-end asset delivery may also involve Google font delivery services and Cloudflare's cdnjs service. Those providers are disclosed in the Privacy Policy because they may receive technical request data even when no non-essential tracking category is enabled.
