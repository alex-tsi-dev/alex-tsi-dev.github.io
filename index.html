Process

A practical path from problem to shipped work

The work usually starts with context, current constraints, and the next outcome the team needs. From there, the focus moves into scope, delivery, and a sensible handoff or next phase.

01

Discovery and context

We look at the product goal, the current bottleneck, and the technical context behind the work.

02

Review and scope

I define the practical scope, surface the important technical decisions, and line up the delivery approach.

03

Build and delivery

Implementation moves through backend delivery, integrations, release support, and team feedback with attention to maintainability.

04

Handover and next step

You get a release-ready outcome, clear technical handoff, and a sensible recommendation for what should happen next.

FAQ

Questions teams usually ask before starting

What does Alex TSI do?

I help product teams build, modernize, and ship backend-heavy software with .NET engineering, DevOps support, cloud delivery, and technical audits.

Who is this best for?

Product teams, SaaS companies, and businesses with backend systems that are growing, slowing down delivery, or overdue for cleanup.

Can you join an existing team?

Yes. I can work inside an existing team, take ownership of a defined scope, or start with an audit when the team needs technical clarity first.

Do you offer technical audits?

Yes. Technical audits help clarify architecture decisions, modernization priorities, and the best implementation path before a larger build, release, or delivery phase begins.

What technologies do you work with most?

The core stack includes .NET, ASP.NET Core, C#, SQL, Docker, Kubernetes, Azure, AWS, CI/CD, integrations, and messaging-based backend systems.

Where are you based?

I am based in Poland and available for remote collaboration worldwide.

Do you only do backend work?

Backend is the core focus. I also handle CI/CD, cloud work, and practical frontend delivery when it helps ship the product without extra handoffs.

Who is this not for?

This is not the right fit for teams looking only for the lowest hourly rate or for a large agency-style delivery team.
